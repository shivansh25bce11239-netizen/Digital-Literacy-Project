## Social Media Do's & Don'ts for College Students

## Do's:

1.Review your privacy settings: Be sure that your private posts are only being seen by people you want them to be seen by.

2.Think before you post: Remember that what you post will stay online forever. Employers and academic institutions often check social media sites.

3.Use social media sites to network: Be sure to use sites like LinkedIn or Twitter to network with people in your field of interest.

4.Verify the information you post: Be sure to verify news stories or announcements before posting them to ensure that you are not disseminating false information.

5.Be respectful: Engage in healthy and constructive conversations. Be respectful of other people's opinions.



## Don'ts:

1.Oversharing information: Do not share your home address, phone number, or location with people online. This could lead to dangerous situations.

2.Posting inappropriate content: Do not post photos or words that will reflect poorly upon you in an academic or professional environment.

3.Engaging in cyberbullying: Do not participate in online bullying. This includes online harassment, trolling, and disseminating rumors.

4.Venting about college and professors: Do not post about academic frustrations. This will show people that you are unprofessional.

5.Clicking suspicious links: Avoid interacting with unverified links or quizzes, as they often lead to phishing scams or malware.
