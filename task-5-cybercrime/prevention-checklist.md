"Stay Safe Online" Prevention Checklist

-Use Strong Passwords & 2FA: Always use unique and complex passwords for your college portals and personal accounts. Also, use Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) where possible.

-Never Use Public Wi-Fi for Transactions: Do not use public Wi-Fi at cafes or hostels to make fee payments or check your bank account information. Use your mobile data network instead.

-Always Verify Links Received Through Emails: Do not respond to emails that promise highly paid internships or urgent updates from your college. Also, always check the sender's real email address before clicking on any links.

-Secure Your Social Media Account: Do not make your social media account public. Also, do not share your location or daily routine with anyone. Do not accept any stranger's friend request either.

-The Golden UPI Rule: Do not enter your UPI PIN to receive money from anyone. Your 4-digit or 6-digit PIN is only required when you are sending money to someone else or checking your balance.

-Beware whwn using Screen Sharing Apps: Do not download AnyDesk, TeamViewer, or QuickSupport on your phone at the advice of customer care. Such applications are being used by hackers to secretly view your phone screen and steal your UPI PIN.

-Avoid Pirated Software: Pirated software, cracked games, and pirated study materials are often found on untrusted torrent sites and are often a medium for spreading ransomware and malware.

-Keep Devices Updated: Ensure your smartphone and laptop operating systems are updated along with your antivirus programs to prevent any potential vulnerabilities.


Emergency Reporting Channels:
If you or someone you know falls victim to a cybercrime, report it immediately using these official channels:

National Cyber Crime Portal: cybercrime.gov.in 
Cyber Crime Helpline: Dial 1930 (available 24x7)
